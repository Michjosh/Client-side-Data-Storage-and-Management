To find information about your local storage, session storage, and cookies in your browser, you can follow these steps for popular web browsers:

Google Chrome:

Open Google Chrome and run your javascript file on the browser from your IDE to check the storage and cookies.
Right-click anywhere on the page and select "Inspect" from the context menu. Alternatively, you can press Ctrl+Shift+I (or Cmd+Option+I on a Mac) to open the Developer Tools.
In the Developer Tools panel, navigate to the "Application" tab.
In the left sidebar, you will find the "Storage" section. Click on "Local Storage" or "Session Storage" to view the data stored in those respective storage mechanisms.
To view cookies, expand the "Cookies" section in the left sidebar. Here, you can see the cookies associated with the current website.

Mozilla Firefox:

Open Mozilla Firefox and run your javascript file on the browser from your IDE.
Right-click anywhere on the page and select "Inspect Element" from the context menu. Alternatively, you can press Ctrl+Shift+I (or Cmd+Option+I on a Mac) to open the Developer Tools.
In the Developer Tools panel, click on the "Storage" tab.
In the left sidebar, you will find options for "Cookies", "Local Storage", and "Session Storage". Click on the desired option to view the corresponding data.

Microsoft Edge:

Open Microsoft Edge and run your javascript file on the browser from your IDE.
Right-click anywhere on the page and select "Inspect Element" from the context menu. Alternatively, you can press F12 to open the Developer Tools.
In the Developer Tools panel, navigate to the "Storage" tab.
In the left sidebar, you will find options for "Cookies", "Local Storage", and "Session Storage". Click on the desired option to view the corresponding data.

These instructions may vary slightly depending on the browser versions or updates, but generally, the steps mentioned above should help you access the storage and cookie information in your browser.
